The Convergence

These are not nine separate crises. They are nine measurements of the same crisis, taken from different angles. And they converge on the same window: the late 1970s through the early 1980s — the precise moment when the nation, confronted with real problems that demanded institutional adaptation, chose instead to dismantle the institutions.

The experiment has been running for half a century. The results are in. By every quantifiable metric — longevity, equality, literacy, trust, democratic function, physical health, civic cohesion — America is worse off than it was when the experiment began, and worse off than every comparable nation that chose a different path.
